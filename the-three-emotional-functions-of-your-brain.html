The three emotional functions of your brain

OdinAangemaakt op:10th May 2020 at 3:54pm - Laatst geëdit op:26th April 2021 at 6:33pm
Zettel

Expecting (anticipating), eagerness (wanting), and enjoying (liking) are separate functions in your brain. You can want without liking (craving), anticipate without wanting (dread), or any other combination.

These mechanisms form the universal mammalian hardware of pleasure, learing, and motivation.

The enjoying system is the part that says “Yes!” or “No!” in your brain. It assesses the hedonistic impact of a stimulus: Does it feel good? How bad?

Expecting is the process of linking what's happening now with what should come next. This is formed by implicit learning. The expecting system is linking stimuli across time and space.

Eagerness, the third system, is the generic gas pedal of the emotional brain. Eagerness fuels the desire to move toward something or away from it. The exerience of craving of yearning is powered by the eagerness system. But also searching for safety when it is activated with the stress response system. Or affection, when it is activated with the attachment mechanism.
